Here are some of the best things to see in each of the cities you mentioned:

Bologna:
- Piazza Maggiore: The main square of Bologna, surrounded by historical buildings and the beautiful Basilica di San Petronio.
- Two Towers (Due Torri): Iconic leaning towers of Bologna offering panoramic views of the city.
- Basilica of Santo Stefano: A complex of seven interconnected churches with a rich history.
- Archiginnasio of Bologna: Former main building of the University of Bologna, known for its stunning anatomical theater.

Pisa:
- Piazza dei Miracoli: Home to the famous Leaning Tower of Pisa, along with the Duomo (Cathedral), Baptistery, and Camposanto Monumentale.
- Take a stroll along the Arno River and explore the picturesque streets of the city.

Siena:
- Piazza del Campo: The main square of Siena, known for its unique shell-shaped design and the Palazzo Pubblico.
- Siena Cathedral (Duomo di Siena): A magnificent Gothic cathedral with stunning artwork and a panoramic view from the top.
- Museo dell'Opera Metropolitana: Houses important religious artworks from Siena's cathedral and other churches.

Perugia:
- Rocca Paolina: An ancient fortress and city underground, providing a fascinating glimpse into Perugia's history.
- Palazzo dei Priori: A beautiful Gothic palace housing the Galleria Nazionale dell'Umbria, showcasing art from the region.
- Corso Vannucci: The main street of Perugia, lined with shops, cafes, and historic buildings.

Rome:
- Colosseum: The iconic ancient amphitheater and symbol of Rome's grandeur.
- Roman Forum: The political and commercial center of ancient Rome, with ruins of temples, basilicas, and markets.
- Vatican City: Explore St. Peter's Basilica, the Vatican Museums (including the Sistine Chapel), and enjoy the view from the top of St. Peter's Dome.
- Trevi Fountain: A stunning Baroque fountain where visitors can toss a coin and make a wish.

Pompeii:
- Pompeii Archaeological Park: Explore the remarkably preserved ruins of this ancient Roman city destroyed by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD.
- Villa of the Mysteries: A well-preserved Roman villa known for its beautiful frescoes.
- Amphitheatre of Pompeii: One of the oldest surviving Roman amphitheaters, offering insight into ancient entertainment.

Amalfi (Positano, Ravello):
- Amalfi Cathedral (Cattedrale di Sant'Andrea): A striking cathedral with intricate architecture and stunning coastal views.
- Positano: Explore the charming village with its colorful houses, narrow streets, and beautiful beaches.
- Ravello: Visit Villa Rufolo and Villa Cimbrone for their stunning gardens and panoramic views of the Amalfi Coast.

Naples:
- Naples National Archaeological Museum: Houses a vast collection of artifacts from Pompeii and Herculaneum, including ancient sculptures and frescoes.
- Historic Center (Spaccanapoli): Explore the bustling streets, visit San Gregorio Armeno for its famous nativity scenes, and try authentic Neapolitan pizza.
- Castel dell'Ovo: A seaside castle offering panoramic views of Naples and the Bay of Naples.

Sorrento:
- Piazza Tasso: The main square of Sorrento, surrounded by shops, cafes, and historic buildings.
- Marina Grande: A charming fishing village with colorful houses and seaside restaurants.
- Explore the narrow streets of the historic center and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Bay of Naples.
