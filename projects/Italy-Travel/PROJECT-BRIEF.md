## Recommendations

### From Destination Research

**Pisa:**
- A day trip is sufficient; main attraction is Piazza dei Miracoli (Leaning Tower, Cathedral, Baptistery)

**Perugia vs San Gimignano:**
- Perugia: larger city, cultural/museum focus, university atmosphere, Umbrian cuisine
- San Gimignano: smaller medieval tower town, more intimate, wine (Vernaccia), UNESCO site
- Decision: San Gimignano area was explored for winery research (for a friend's trip)

**Sicily (from 2023 research):**
- Recommended 7-10 days minimum for Palermo, Catania, Taormina, Ragusa
- Car travel preferred for flexibility in rural areas; train better for major cities
- Naples to Sicily: flying is fastest (~1h), ferry is scenic (6-12h), train requires Strait of Messina crossing

**City Visit Durations (from research):**
- Florence (excluding museums): 3-4 days
- Rome (excluding museums): 3-4 days
- Bologna (excluding museums): 2 days
- Siena: 1 full day
- Perugia: 1-2 days
- Pisa: 1 day (day trip sufficient)
- Naples: 2-3 days
- Amalfi Coast: 2-3 days

### Top Winery Recommendations (from research conversations)

**For Chianti Classico (friend's trip near San Gimignano/Castellina):**
1. Castello di Fonterutoli (Mazzei family, since 1435) -- iconic, Castellina area
2. Castello di Ama -- world-class, wine + contemporary art, Gaiole area
3. Castellare di Castellina -- terroir-focused, excellent Sangiovese, directly in Castellina

**Bolgheri Alternatives (if Ornellaia unavailable):**
1. Tenuta San Guido (Sassicaia) -- legendary, highest prestige, very limited visits
2. Guado al Tasso (Antinori's Bolgheri estate) -- complements Tignanello visit
3. Le Macchiole -- boutique, top-rated (Messorio, Paleo, Scrio)
4. Argentiera -- elegant, hillside, sea views

**Brunello Alternatives (if Valdicava unavailable):**
1. Casanova di Neri -- cult modern producer (Tenuta Nuova, Cerretalto)
2. Altesino -- pioneers of single-vineyard Brunello (Montosoli)
3. Caparzo -- historic, approachable, easy to book
4. Soldera (Case Basse) -- ultra-iconic but extremely hard to arrange
5. Biondi-Santi -- iconic heritage but does NOT offer tours/tastings (closed to public)
