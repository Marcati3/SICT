# 1. Master Service Agreement

## MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Master Service Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** by and between:

1. **[Thai Partner Company Name]**, a company incorporated under the laws of the Kingdom of Thailand, having its registered office at **[Address]** (“Thai Partner”); and

2. **[Client Legal Name]**, a company incorporated under the laws of **[Jurisdiction]**, having its registered office at **[Address]** (“Client”).

Thai Partner and Client are hereinafter collectively referred to as the “Parties” and individually as a “Party”.

### 1. Purpose and Structure

1.1 This Agreement sets out the terms under which Thai Partner will provide governance, corporate, regulatory, and representation services in connection with **[Name of Thai Entity]** (the “Thai Entity”) and other agreed services in Thailand.

1.2 The detailed scope of services for each service line, including IOR/EOR, PDPA Representation, IP Custody, Product Registration, Franchise Support, and others, shall be set out in one or more **Statements of Work or Service Schedules** (each, an “SOW” or “Schedule”), attached as Schedules **B–F** (and as may be updated from time to time). Each SOW forms an integral part of this Agreement.

### 2. Governance, Substance, and Management Control

2.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the Thai Partner shall exercise **genuine management control** over the Thai Entity, in accordance with applicable Thai law and the approved governance framework.

2.2 All decisions requiring corporate approval, including but not limited to changes in company objectives, capital structure, director appointments and removals, opening or closing bank accounts, and material contractual commitments, shall be made by the Thai Partner’s designated director(s) **in accordance with the delegation matrix** attached as **Schedule A (Delegation of Authority Matrix)**.

2.3 There shall be an **active board of directors** for the Thai Entity. The Thai Partner shall ensure that:

a. Board and shareholder meetings are held with appropriate frequency as specified in Schedule A.  
b. Minutes are prepared and maintained in both English and Thai where required.  
c. A defined **signing authority schedule** is implemented and kept current.  
d. A documented **dividend and distribution policy** exists and is implemented, demonstrating genuine economic substance, including actual dividends or reinvestment in operations as appropriate.

### 3. Scope of Services

3.1 Thai Partner may provide one or more of the following services (collectively, the “Services”):

a. Corporate secretarial and governance services.  
b. Thai director and/or Thai shareholder participation.  
c. Importer of Record / Exporter of Record (“IOR/EOR”) services.  
d. PDPA local representative and data protection support services.  
e. Product registration and applicant-of-record services (NBTC, TISI, DIW, and similar authorities).  
f. Trademark and IP filing, custody, and license-back services.  
g. Franchise support and local partner / joint-venture participation.  
h. Other governance, compliance, or regulatory-related services as agreed in an SOW.

3.2 Each SOW shall define, at a minimum:

a. Scope and deliverables.  
b. Service levels and key performance indicators (SLAs).  
c. Timelines and acceptance criteria.  
d. Fee structure, payment milestones, and applicable taxes.  
e. Any specific compliance, reporting, or audit obligations.

### 4. Term and Termination

4.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and remain in force until terminated in accordance with this Clause.

4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’** written notice to the other Party, unless otherwise specified in a particular SOW.

4.3 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any affected SOW, with immediate effect by written notice if the other Party:

a. Commits a material breach of this Agreement or any SOW and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice.  
b. Becomes insolvent, enters bankruptcy, or ceases business.  
c. Is subject to sanctions or regulatory actions that materially affect its ability to perform under this Agreement.

### 5. Termination Assistance and Transition

5.1 Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement or any SOW, Thai Partner shall provide reasonable **transition assistance** to minimize disruption to the Thai Entity’s operations, including:

a. Cooperation in the appointment and registration of replacement directors, shareholders, or local representatives.  
b. Transfer of relevant corporate records, filings, and documentation to Client or a successor.  
c. Assistance with closing or transferring regulatory approvals, licenses, and registrations where feasible.

5.2 Thai Partner shall, upon written request and subject to full payment of all outstanding fees:

a. **Return or destroy** (at Client’s option) Client’s data, subject to any legal retention obligations.  
b. Facilitate the transfer or assignment of any IP or trademark registrations held in custody, in accordance with the applicable IP Custody Addendum.  
c. Provide a final governance and compliance summary report for the services rendered, if agreed in an SOW.

5.3 Client shall cooperate fully with Thai Partner to effect a smooth and lawful transition and shall bear all official fees and third-party costs of transition.

### 6. Fees and Payment

6.1 Fees for each Service shall be as set out in the applicable SOW. Fees may include:

a. Fixed setup fees.  
b. Recurring retainers (monthly, quarterly, or annually).  
c. Transaction-based or value-based fees, such as a percentage of shipment value for IOR/EOR.  
d. Escrow deposits or security retainers for regulatory risks.  
e. Official and third-party fees, such as customs duties, lab fees, legal opinions.

6.2 Unless otherwise agreed in an SOW:

a. All fees are exclusive of VAT and other applicable taxes.  
b. Fees are payable **in advance** or in accordance with milestones stated in the SOW.  
c. Invoices are due within **[15/30]** days from the invoice date.

6.3 Failure to pay on time may result in suspension of Services and, after written notice, termination of the relevant SOW.

### 7. Compliance Covenants

7.1 **PDPA and Data Protection.** Both Parties shall comply with applicable data protection laws, including the Thai Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (2019) (“PDPA”) and any implementing regulations. The Parties shall enter into additional data processing or PDPA representative agreements where required by law or the SOW.

7.2 **Anti-Corruption.** Each Party agrees that it shall not, directly or indirectly, offer, give, solicit, or accept any bribe, improper payment, or advantage in connection with this Agreement. Each Party shall comply with all applicable anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws and policies.

7.3 **Sanctions Compliance.** Client represents and warrants that it, its owners, and its key officers are not subject to sanctions lists, including UN, EU, and OFAC. Client shall not cause Thai Partner to engage with any sanctioned entities or in any sanctioned activities.

7.4 **AML / KYC.** Client acknowledges that Thai Partner must conduct KYC/AML checks and may request additional documentation at onboarding and periodically thereafter. Client agrees to provide accurate, complete information and to notify Thai Partner of any material changes. Thai Partner may refuse or terminate Services where AML/KYC concerns arise.

7.5 **Audit and Inspection Rights.** Thai Partner shall maintain documentation evidencing governance, substance, and key compliance activities, including board minutes, resolutions, and approval workflows. Subject to reasonable notice and confidentiality, Client may request:

a. A summary of governance records maintained for the Thai Entity.  
b. An internal compliance review meeting.  
c. Access to certain underlying records where necessary to satisfy external auditors or regulators.

### 8. Liability and Indemnities

8.1 **Liability Cap.** Except as set out in Clauses 8.3 and 8.4, each Party’s aggregate liability to the other Party arising out of or in connection with this Agreement and all SOWs, whether in contract, tort, negligence, or otherwise, shall be limited to the greater of:

a. The total fees paid or payable by Client to Thai Partner under the relevant SOW during the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim.  
b. **[X] times** the annual contract value for the relevant SOW.

8.2 In no event shall either Party be liable for:

a. Indirect, consequential, or special damages.  
b. Loss of profit, revenue, or anticipated savings.  
c. Loss of business or goodwill.

8.3 The limitations in Clause 8.1 shall **not** apply to:

a. Willful misconduct or fraud.  
b. Gross negligence causing material damage or regulatory sanctions.  
c. Breach of confidentiality obligations under this Agreement.  
d. Data breaches resulting from a Party’s failure to implement agreed security measures.  
e. IP infringement where a Party knowingly provides infringing materials.

8.4 **Mutual Indemnities.**

a. Client shall indemnify and hold harmless Thai Partner, its directors, officers, and representatives from claims, penalties, and costs arising out of:

   i. Client’s regulatory non-compliance, misrepresentations, or incomplete documentation.  
   ii. Inaccurate or misleading customs or product data.  
   iii. Client’s data privacy violations.  
   iv. Infringement of third-party IP rights based on materials supplied by Client.

b. Thai Partner shall indemnify and hold harmless Client from claims, penalties, and costs arising out of:

   i. Thai Partner’s willful misconduct or fraud.  
   ii. Thai Partner’s unauthorized disclosure of Client’s Confidential Information.  
   iii. Thai Partner’s failure to comply with PDPA or other data protection obligations specifically allocated to Thai Partner.  
   iv. IP infringement caused by Thai Partner’s own proprietary materials, if any, supplied to Client.

8.5 **Insurance.** Each Party shall maintain commercially reasonable insurance coverage, including professional indemnity and cyber/data breach coverage where relevant, with reputable insurers, and shall provide evidence of such coverage upon reasonable request.

### 9. Confidentiality and Data Handling

9.1 Each Party shall treat all non-public information of the other Party as confidential and shall protect it using reasonable technical and organizational measures.

9.2 Data handling, retention, and destruction shall follow:

a. The PDPA and applicable laws.  
b. The data handling and retention policies referenced in the PDPA Representative Addendum and the KYC/KYB forms.  
c. Any additional instructions in an SOW.

9.3 Upon expiry or termination, data shall be returned or securely destroyed in accordance with Clause 5 and the relevant SOW, subject to legal retention requirements.

### 10. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

10.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Kingdom of Thailand.

10.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall first be discussed in good faith between senior representatives of both Parties.

10.3 If no resolution is reached within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be submitted to the competent courts of Thailand, which shall have exclusive jurisdiction.

### 11. Miscellaneous

11.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement together with all SOWs and Schedules constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties.

11.2 Amendments. Any amendment must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

11.3 Assignment. Client shall not assign this Agreement or any SOW without Thai Partner’s prior written consent.

11.4 Severability. If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

11.5 Language. This Agreement is executed in English. A Thai translation may be prepared for local authorities; in case of conflict, the English version prevails as between the Parties.

# 3. Addendum B — IOR / EOR Agreement

## ADDENDUM B – IMPORTER / EXPORTER OF RECORD (IOR/EOR) AGREEMENT

This IOR/EOR Addendum (“Addendum”) is made pursuant to the Master Service Agreement between **[Thai Partner]** and **[Client]**.

### 1. Nature of the Arrangement

1.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that this Addendum establishes a **compliance-based IOR/EOR arrangement** and **does not constitute a nominee structure** or any arrangement intended to circumvent Thai foreign business restrictions.

1.2 Thai Partner’s role as IOR/EOR is grounded in regulatory good practice and substance, including proper governance records, customs documentation, and cooperation with licensed customs brokers.

### 2. Scope of IOR/EOR Services

2.1 Subject to this Addendum, Thai Partner may act as:

a. Importer of Record (IOR) for specified shipments into Thailand.  
b. Exporter of Record (EOR) for outbound shipments from Thailand.

In each case only where Thai Partner has issued a written acceptance for the specific shipment.

2.2 Thai Partner will:

a. Coordinate with licensed customs brokers.  
b. Submit required customs documentation.  
c. Maintain all necessary records, including invoices, packing lists, entries, and approvals, in line with Thai law.  
d. Support post-clearance audits as reasonably required.

2.3 Where applicable, the Parties will also ensure consideration of:

a. Labor and immigration compliance for any Thai-resident personnel deployed under this arrangement.  
b. Visa and work permit compliance if any foreign staff are involved in on-the-ground operations for the Thai Entity.  
c. Coordination with other regulators if the goods fall under special regimes, such as NBTC, DIW, or TISI.

### 3. Client Responsibilities

3.1 Client shall provide, for each shipment:

a. Accurate commercial invoice and packing list.  
b. Correct HS codes and detailed product descriptions.  
c. Country of origin and certificates where required.  
d. All licenses, permits, test reports, or approvals required for the product.  
e. Any other documents requested by Thai Partner or customs broker.

3.2 Client warrants that:

a. Goods are not prohibited, restricted, or sanctioned.  
b. Goods comply with all safety, labeling, and standards obligations.  
c. All declarations are accurate and complete.

3.3 Client acknowledges that Thai Partner is not responsible for verifying product technical content or the truthfulness of information supplied by Client.

### 4. Compliance Guardrails

4.1 The Parties expressly agree that:

a. Thai Partner will not hold goods as inventory owner for commercial profit.  
b. Thai Partner will not engage in sham or fictitious arrangements designed to disguise the identity of the actual beneficial owner.  
c. All IOR/EOR activities must be supported by real substance and documentation, including board minutes, resolutions, contracts, and bank signatories where applicable.

4.2 Thai Partner shall maintain evidence of substance, including:

a. Board approvals or delegated authority for IOR/EOR activities.  
b. Shipment-level documentation.  
c. Records of interactions with customs and regulators.  
d. Internal compliance notes and risk assessments.

4.3 Any arrangement that might reasonably be construed as **nominee governance** or as an attempt to evade Thai law is strictly prohibited. Thai Partner may immediately refuse or cease services if such concerns arise.

### 5. Fees, Insurance, and Fines

5.1 Fees for IOR/EOR services, including fixed retainers and variable shipment fees, are specified in the applicable SOW.

5.2 Client shall maintain adequate insurance coverage for:

a. Transit and cargo risks.  
b. Product liability.  
c. Recall where applicable.  
d. Cover for customs duty shortfall or errors, if available.

5.3 Thai Partner shall maintain suitable professional indemnity coverage respecting its IOR/EOR role, within commercially reasonable limits.

5.4 Allocation of fines and penalties:

a. Fines arising from Client’s inaccurate or incomplete information, misclassification, under-valuation, or compliance failures shall be borne by Client.  
b. Fines arising solely from Thai Partner’s gross negligence or willful misconduct will be borne by Thai Partner, subject to the liability cap in the Agreement.  
c. Where both Parties contribute, fines will be apportioned based on relative responsibility.

### 6. Indemnity

6.1 Client shall indemnify Thai Partner and its officers, directors, and employees against claims, penalties, and costs arising from:

a. Incorrect or incomplete documentation.  
b. Mis-declared HS codes or values.  
c. Non-compliant products.  
d. Violations of sanctions, export controls, or other international trade rules caused by Client’s actions.

6.2 Thai Partner shall indemnify Client for losses directly resulting from Thai Partner’s willful misconduct, fraud, or gross negligence in executing its IOR/EOR responsibilities.

### 7. Termination and Transition

7.1 Either Party may terminate this Addendum in accordance with the Agreement.

7.2 Upon termination or expiry:

a. Thai Partner will cooperate to ensure a smooth transition to a successor IOR/EOR or to a Client-controlled Thai entity.  
b. Thai Partner will provide shipment and customs records reasonably required to support post-transition compliance.  
c. Client shall settle all outstanding fees and reimburse any customs or duty liabilities incurred.

7.3 Thai Partner shall retain IOR/EOR records for the period required by Thai law, after which they shall be securely destroyed unless otherwise agreed.

# 4. Addendum C — PDPA Local Representative Agreement

## ADDENDUM C – PDPA LOCAL REPRESENTATIVE AGREEMENT

This PDPA Addendum (“Addendum”) is made pursuant to the Master Service Agreement between **[Thai Partner]** and **[Client]**.

### 1. Roles and Definitions

1.1 For purposes of this Addendum:

- “PDPA” means the Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (2019) of Thailand and its implementing regulations.  
- “Controller”, “Processor”, and “Data Subject” have the meanings given in PDPA.

1.2 Client shall be the **data controller** and/or **data processor** in respect of personal data of Thai Data Subjects that it collects or processes in connection with its business.

1.3 Thai Partner is appointed as **local representative** and, where agreed, a **limited data processor** only for the purposes specified in this Addendum and relevant SOWs.

### 2. Scope of PDPA Representative Services

Thai Partner shall:

a. Act as the official contact point in Thailand for Data Subjects and regulators, including the PDPC.  
b. Receive and forward PDPA-related inquiries, complaints, and notices.  
c. Maintain an incident and request log.  
d. Provide reasonable support to Client in understanding Thai regulatory correspondence.  
e. Coordinate local actions required by regulators, in consultation with Client.

### 3. Data Processing and Data Handling

3.1 Thai Partner shall only process personal data on documented instructions from Client, unless required by Thai law.

3.2 Thai Partner shall implement appropriate **technical and organizational measures** to protect personal data, including access controls, encryption where appropriate, and secure transmission and storage.

3.3 Data retention:

a. Thai Partner will retain personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill its obligations or as required by Thai law.  
b. At the end of engagement, personal data shall be either securely deleted or returned to Client, subject to legal retention requirements.

3.4 Data access and deletion:

a. Client may instruct Thai Partner to correct or delete specific personal data where PDPA requires.  
b. Thai Partner shall act on such instructions within the time frame agreed in the relevant SOW or within a reasonable period.

### 4. Data Subject Rights

4.1 For data subject requests, including access, correction, deletion, objection, and portability:

a. Thai Partner shall log the request within one (1) business day of receipt.  
b. Thai Partner shall notify Client without undue delay and in any event within **five (5) business days**.  
c. Client remains responsible for substantive decisions on the request.  
d. Thai Partner will, upon instructions, support communication with the data subject.

4.2 The Parties shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that data subject requests are resolved within the timeframes required by PDPA.

### 5. Data Breach Notification and Incident Response

5.1 In the event Thai Partner becomes aware of a personal data breach affecting personal data processed under this Addendum, Thai Partner shall:

a. Notify Client without undue delay and in any event within **seventy-two (72) hours** of confirmation of the breach.  
b. Provide available details of the breach, its impact, and known or suspected causes.  
c. Cooperate with Client in investigating and remediating the breach.  
d. Support Client in preparing any required notifications to regulators or data subjects.

5.2 Client remains responsible for deciding whether and how to notify regulators and data subjects, in compliance with PDPA.

### 6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

6.1 Where personal data of Thai Data Subjects is transferred from Thailand to another jurisdiction, Client shall ensure the use of adequate transfer mechanisms under PDPA, including contractual clauses, adequacy, or other lawful bases.

6.2 Thai Partner will only transfer personal data outside Thailand upon documented instructions from Client and shall cooperate to implement any standard contractual clauses or equivalent measures required.

### 7. Audit and Compliance

7.1 Client may, on reasonable notice, request a summary of Thai Partner’s data protection controls relating to services under this Addendum.

7.2 If required by regulators, Parties shall cooperate in providing evidence of compliance with PDPA, including logs, policies, and agreements.

### 8. Liability

8.1 Thai Partner’s liability relating to PDPA services shall be subject to the liability limitations and carve-outs in the Agreement, with special attention to data breach and confidentiality exceptions.

8.2 Each Party shall indemnify the other for PDPA penalties and damages caused by its own non-compliance, subject to the agreed liability caps.

# 6. Addendum E — Trademark and IP Custody with License-Back

## ADDENDUM E – TRADEMARK AND IP CUSTODY WITH LICENSE-BACK

This IP Custody Addendum (“Addendum”) is made pursuant to the Master Service Agreement between **[Thai Partner]** and **[Client]**.

### 1. Ownership vs. Custody

1.1 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Client remains the **beneficial owner** of all intellectual property rights in its trademarks, logos, and related brand assets.

1.2 For regulatory or commercial convenience, certain trademarks may be filed and held in the name of Thai Partner or another designated Thai entity as **custodian**.

1.3 Thai Partner’s holding of such registrations shall not be construed as a transfer of beneficial ownership, except where a separate IP assignment agreement is executed.

### 2. License-Back Arrangement

2.1 Thai Partner hereby grants to Client a **license-back** to use the trademark(s) in Thailand for the agreed products or services.

2.2 The license shall specify:

a. **Field of use**, such as bakery outlets, restaurant services, or retail of goods.  
b. **Territory**, meaning Thailand and any agreed sub-territories.  
c. **Duration**, such as co-terminous with the MSA or a specific term of years.  
d. Whether royalty-free or subject to an agreed **royalty rate**, such as X% of net revenue in Thailand.  
e. Sub-licensing rules, if any.

2.3 Upon termination:

a. License may be wound down over an agreed **wind-down period**, such as 6–12 months, to allow orderly replacement of signage, packaging, and other branded assets.  
b. Parties may negotiate assignment of registrations to Client or a new Thai entity upon agreed terms.  
c. Client shall cease further use after the wind-down period unless otherwise agreed.

### 3. Duration, Renewal, and Revocation

3.1 The trademark license shall commence on the date of registration or as specified in the SOW and continue for the duration of the MSA, unless terminated earlier.

3.2 Renewal of trademark registrations will be managed by Thai Partner, with Client responsible for renewal fees and service charges.

3.3 Thai Partner may suspend or revoke the license in case of:

a. Non-payment of fees or royalties.  
b. Serious breach of brand guidelines.  
c. Violation of applicable laws.  
d. Termination of the franchise or local partner relationship, if applicable.

### 4. Security and Confidentiality

4.1 Thai Partner shall implement reasonable security measures to protect IP-related assets and documentation, including:

a. Restricted access to trademark portfolios.  
b. Secure digital storage with encryption as appropriate.  
c. Controls for who can authorize filings, renewals, or assignments.

4.2 Any incident compromising IP assets, such as unauthorized assignments, registry errors, or significant infringements discovered, shall be promptly notified to Client, and the Parties shall cooperate in remediation.

### 5. Infringement and Enforcement

5.1 Client shall inform Thai Partner of any suspected infringement in Thailand.

5.2 Parties shall coordinate on enforcement strategy, considering costs, risks, and brand priorities.

5.3 Cost-sharing and decision-making for enforcement actions shall be detailed in the relevant SOW.

# 10. Client Acceptance / Rejection Matrix and Workflow

## CLIENT ACCEPTANCE & REJECTION MATRIX AND WORKFLOW

### Section A: Objective Acceptance Criteria

**Low-Risk / Accept**
- Jurisdiction: US/EU/UK/AU/NZ or other low-risk jurisdictions  
- Transparent ownership, full KYC/KYB complete  
- Products: non-hazardous, non-regulated consumer or industrial goods  
- No sanctions, serious adverse media, or history of regulatory fines  

**Medium-Risk / Conditional Accept**
- Jurisdiction includes some medium-risk countries but with clear structure  
- Products involve RF/telecom, IoT, or higher compliance complexity  
- Minor historical compliance issues, resolved and documented  

**High-Risk / Reject**
- Links to sanctioned or high-risk jurisdictions  
- Products in prohibited or sensitive categories (medical, chemicals, narcotics, gambling, crypto, weapons, cannabis, etc.)  
- Nominee-style requests or explicit attempts to circumvent Thai law  
- Refusal to provide adequate KYC/KYB documentation  

### Section B: Formal Review Workflow

1. **Initial Assessment**
   - Completed by: Compliance Coordinator  
   - Inputs: Client Intake Form, KYC/KYB, website review, media check  

2. **Risk Scoring**
   - Tool: Internal AML/CFT risk matrix  
   - Output: Low / Medium / High  

3. **Decision**
   - Low-risk: Approve at Compliance Coordinator level  
   - Medium-risk: Escalate to **Senior Compliance / Management** for approval  
   - High-risk: Escalate to **Thai Partner Director** for final decision; default stance is reject unless exceptional circumstances  

4. **Documentation**
   - Decision recorded in a **Client Risk File**  
   - Attach supporting documents and rationale  

### Section C: Documentation & Retention

For each acceptance or rejection decision, maintain:

- Completed KYC/KYB form  
- Risk scoring sheet  
- Any enhanced due diligence reports  
- Internal email or notes documenting the reasoning  
- Final decision form signed by approver  

Retention:
- Keep records for **at least [5–10] years** after the end of the relationship.

### Section D: Escalation Path

- Compliance Coordinator → Senior Compliance / Legal → Thai Partner Director

Any disagreement or borderline case must go to the highest level before an accept or reject decision is finalized.
