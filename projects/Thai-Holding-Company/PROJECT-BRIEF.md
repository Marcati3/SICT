# Thai Holding Company — Project Brief

## Project Overview

A personal project to establish a Thailand-based holding company that provides governance, corporate, regulatory, and representation services to **Western SMEs entering the Thai market**. The business operates on a compliance-first model, explicitly prohibiting nominee arrangements and requiring genuine management control and substance.

The target client is a Western SME (US/EU/UK/AU/NZ) that needs a compliant Thai presence — whether for importing goods, registering products, protecting IP, collecting Thai personal data, or establishing a franchise — without setting up their own full Thai entity and compliance infrastructure.

---

## Key Decisions Made

### Service Model Architecture

1. **Comprehensive service suite designed**, covering:
   - Importer of Record / Exporter of Record (IOR/EOR)
   - PDPA local representative services
   - Trademark/IP custody and license-back arrangements
   - Product registration (NBTC, TISI, DIW)
   - Thai director/shareholder participation
   - Corporate secretarial and governance
   - Franchise support and local partner/JV participation

2. **SOW-based delivery model**: Each client engagement is governed by a Master Service Agreement (MSA) with service-specific Statements of Work (SOWs) as Schedules A-F.

### Legal Document Suite

3. **Full legal document suite drafted with significant redline revisions**, incorporating:
   - Genuine management control and substance requirements
   - Liability caps tied to contract value (excluding willful misconduct/gross negligence)
   - PDPA compliance, anti-corruption, sanctions, AML/KYC covenants
   - 72-hour breach notification for data security
   - SOW-based service delivery with SLAs and acceptance criteria
   - Wind-down and transition procedures
   - Explicit prohibition on nominee arrangements

   Documents completed:
   - Master Service Agreement (MSA)
   - Addendum A — Thai Director/Shareholder Participation
   - Addendum B — IOR/EOR Agreement
   - Addendum C — PDPA Local Representative Agreement
   - Addendum D — Product Registration (NBTC/TISI/DIW)
   - Addendum E — Trademark and IP Custody with License-Back
   - KYC/KYB Form
   - Client Intake Form
   - Onboarding and Compliance Document Tracker
   - Client Acceptance/Rejection Matrix and Workflow

### Client Risk Tiers

4. **Three-tier client risk classification defined:**
   - **Green (Ideal):** Western SMEs, clean KYC, non-hazardous goods, compliant
   - **Yellow (Borderline):** Early-stage startups, complex but legal products — requires additional docs and higher fees
   - **Red (Reject):** Medical/supplements/chemicals/crypto/cannabis, incomplete KYC, sanctions history, nominee-seeking

### Compliance Framework

5. **Compliance-first operating model**, including:
   - AML/CFT risk scoring in KYC/KYB process
   - Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) triggers for PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex structures
   - Escalation path: Compliance Coordinator > Senior Compliance/Legal > Thai Partner Director
   - Record retention: 5-10 years after end of relationship
   - PDPA privacy notices embedded in all client-facing forms

## Service Model Details

### IOR/EOR Only
- Thai Partner acts as IOR/EOR via Thai entity without warehouse or operations
- Governance: Board approval for IOR/EOR activities, documented delegation, customs broker agreements
- Control: Thai Partner entity acts as legal importer; Client retains beneficial ownership of goods
- Risk: Customs misdeclaration, sanctions, product compliance
- Regulations: Customs Act, FBA, AML, sanctions

### Full Thai Holding & Governance
- Thai entity with active board, Thai director, PDPA representative, and product registrations
- Governance: Active board, minutes, signing authority schedule, dividend policy
- Control: Thai Partner exercises genuine management control; Client participates under agreed JV structure
- Risk: FBA scrutiny, substance requirements
- Regulations: FBA, CCC, PDPA, AML, Customs, NBTC/TISI/DIW as applicable

## Important Details

### Governance Substance Requirements
- Active board of directors with documented meetings
- Minutes in English and Thai where required
- Defined signing authority schedule
- Documented dividend and distribution policy demonstrating genuine economic substance
- Delegation of Authority Matrix (Schedule A to MSA)
- Annual Conflict of Interest declarations

### PDPA Representative Role
- Act as official contact point in Thailand for Data Subjects and PDPC
- Receive and forward inquiries, complaints, and notices
- Maintain incident and request log
- 72-hour breach notification obligation
- Data subject request logging within 1 business day, Client notification within 5 business days

### IP Custody Model
- Client remains beneficial owner of all IP
- Trademarks may be filed/held by Thai Partner as custodian (not owner)
- License-back to Client for use in Thailand
- License terms: field of use, territory, duration, royalty rate, sub-licensing rules
- Wind-down period (6-12 months) on termination for orderly transition

### Product Registration
- Covers NBTC (RF/telecom/IoT), TISI (electrical/industrial), DIW (chemicals/industrial)
- Thai Partner acts as applicant-of-record
- Renewal calendar with 90/60/30-day advance reminders
- Thai Partner cannot guarantee approval — regulator discretion
