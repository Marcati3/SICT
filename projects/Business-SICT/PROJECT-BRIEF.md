# Business - SICT Project Brief

## Project Overview

This project encompasses the commercial strategy, fundraising activities, HR matters, and legal/quality documentation for an engagement with **Silicon Craft Technology Public Co., Ltd. (SICT)**, a Thai-listed fabless semiconductor company specializing in RFID and NFC integrated circuit design. SICT trades on the Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET) under ticker **SICT**.

The user is a senior commercial executive (international P&L management, sales execution, market development, cross-cultural team leadership) pursuing a strategic role at or alongside SICT to drive revenue growth. The overarching goal is to **double SICT's revenue within 3-5 years**, primarily by strengthening core business segments, expanding into Industrial IoT (IIoT) and smart logistics tracking, and establishing strategic semiconductor alliances.

The **Thailand holding company / IOR-EOR service business** has been moved to its own project (see `~/Claude/Projects/Thai-Holding-Company/`).

## Key Decisions Made

### SICT Commercial Strategy

1. **Growth Target Confirmed**: Double revenue within 3 years (2025-2028), with increased focus on IIoT. Original discussions referenced 5-year doubling; after meeting with SICT leadership, the target was compressed to 3 years.

2. **Tier-2/3 Platform Strategy Approved**: SICT should **not** compete head-to-head as a Tier-1 solution provider in smart logistics tracking. Instead, SICT should operate as a **Tier-2/3 silicon and platform enabler** selling to multiple Tier-1 solution owners. Cellular capability should be treated as an enabler, not a business model change.

3. **Strategic Alliance Model with TI and Others**: Decided to pursue partnerships with Texas Instruments (TI), NXP, STMicroelectronics, and Nordic Semiconductor under the positioning: "We take the projects that are too small, too custom, or too niche for you." This provides deal flow, market visibility, and credibility without heavy sales headcount investment.

4. **Immobilizer Segment Managed Decline**: Acknowledged the immobilizer segment is in sustained decline (~40% drop in FY2024, continued -32% Q1 FY2025 vs Q1 FY2024). Decision to manage this segment rather than reinvest heavily.

5. **Animal ID as Stable Core**: Animal ID remains the largest revenue contributor (~THB 376M in FY2024, ~57% of revenue). Growth strategy focuses on expanding into Europe, Brazil/LatAm, and ASEAN markets through regional tag manufacturers.

6. **Smart Logistics Tracking as Expansion Market**: Detailed customer archetypes developed for tracker OEMs, logistics integrators, and connectivity partners. Named priority accounts identified (see Important Details).

7. **Commodity/FOREX Clause for Allflex**: A price adjustment clause was finalized for quotes to Allflex allowing +/-3% commodity index and FOREX fluctuation adjustments, with methodology agreed pre-PO and 12-month delivery window.

8. **QMA Converted for End Customers**: The supplier quality requirement document was converted into a customer-facing Quality Management Agreement (QMA) between SIC and its end customers, with added governing law (Thailand), precedence clauses, liability flow-down, confidentiality, audit rights, and termination provisions. Compliant with ISO 9001 and ISO 19011.

### HR

9. **Performance Review Clause Drafted**: A clause requiring employees to complete certifications, training, and professional development on their own time and at their own expense was drafted for insertion into performance review documents, with placement recommended after "Certification Goals" section and before "Employee Acknowledgment."

### SICT Product Segments
- **Animal Identification**: RFID transponder ICs for cattle, pigs, sheep, goats. Tags embedded in ear tags, bolus, injectable capsules. Government-mandated programs drive demand.
- **Immobilizers**: RFID/NFC transponder ICs for automotive anti-theft systems (car/motorbike keys). Customers include key/fob manufacturers, aftermarket key duplicators, Tier-2 automotive suppliers. Segment declining due to transition to digital keys (BLE, UWB, smartphone-based).
- **Industrial IoT / Access Control & Interrogator**: RFID/NFC ICs for logistics, manufacturing, access control. Growing segment.
- **NFC & Others**: Early-stage segment including authentication applications.
- **ASIC Design Services**: Custom IC design services (<1% of revenue). Capabilities include front-end architecture, analog/RF design, ASIC layout, simulation, mask generation.

### SICT Founding Story & Ecosystem Role
- **Founded 2002** with NECTEC support for developing ICs for RFID Animal Identification
- Government framework predated SIC: NECTEC established 1986, Reverse Brain Drain program 1997, TIDI incubation unit pre-2001
- SIC's founders took the government-built R&D infrastructure and proved a commercially viable Thai IC design company could succeed — Thailand's first and only fabless IC design house
- Founders brought back international expertise: Silicon Valley (Manop), Imperial College London (Apinunt), Carnegie Mellon (Apinetr)
- Defensible claim: SIC's founders were among the key people who proved Thailand could design chips, not just assemble them, and have since built the industry associations (THSIA, TESA), training infrastructure (KAISEM, NSTCs), and policy frameworks that form Thailand's current semiconductor development ecosystem
- **Government relationship is critical and strategic**: SIC holds BOI A1 Tier certificate (Category 4.1, Electronic Design). SIC leadership is called on to help shape semiconductor policy and structure — this is earned trust built over two decades. SIC's position: true partner and champion of Thailand's semiconductor ambitions. The company exists because of the government's early investment in IC design capability (NECTEC, TIDI, Reverse Brain Drain program) and has repaid that investment by proving Thai IC design is commercially viable. SIC must be seen as a collaborative partner — never adversarial, never critical. The goal is to deepen involvement in policy-shaping, continue to be the private sector voice the government trusts on semiconductor strategy, and demonstrate that Thai-founded companies can compete globally in chip design. Any public commentary supports the government's direction constructively and positions SIC as a company investing alongside government targets, not questioning them.
- **BOI Context**: BOI Category 4.1 (Electronic Design/IC Design) provides A1-tier incentives: 8-year corporate income tax exemption with no cap. Category 4.2 (Semiconductor Fabrication) provides up to A1+ with 13-year tax holidays. BOI is the primary investment promotion agency driving semiconductor FDI alongside the National Semiconductor Board. Recent FDI momentum: Foxsemicon (THB 10.5B), FT1 Corporation SiC wafer fab (THB 11.5B), Infineon backend fab (operational 2026). Thailand saw 35% increase in FDI applications in 2024 to USD 33B. SIC benefits from and contributes to this ecosystem — the company is proof that BOI's IC design incentive structure produces results.
- **National Semiconductor Roadmap 2050**: Three phases — Phase 1 (2026-2030) strengthen OSAT, IC design, advanced electronics; Phase 2 (2031-2040) expand wafer fabrication; Phase 3 (2041-2050) achieve "Made-in-Thailand Chips." Total investment target: THB 2.5 trillion over 25 years. Workforce target: 230,000+ people trained for the industry. SIC is positioned in the highest-value segment (IC design/upstream) from day one — exactly where the roadmap says Thailand needs to go.
