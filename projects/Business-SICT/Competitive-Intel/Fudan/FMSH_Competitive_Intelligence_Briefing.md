## 1. Executive Summary

**Subject:** Shanghai Fudan Microelectronics Group --- FY2024 Strategic Position & Implications for SIC
**Stock Codes:** SSE STAR Market 688385.SH (A-shares) | HKEX 01385.HK (H-shares)
**Revenue:** RMB 3,590M (~USD 495M). +1.5% YoY. Revenue stabilizing after 2023 trough.
**Employees:** 2,077 (as of 31 Dec 2024). 1,130 in R&D (54.4% of workforce).
**Headquarters:** No. 220, Handan Road, Shanghai, PRC. HK office in Tsimshatsui East.
**Business Model:** Fabless IC design --- same model as SIC. Uses 39 outsourced production suppliers (38 domestic, 1 overseas).
**Chairman:** Mr. Jiang Guoxing | **Managing Director:** Mr. Shi Lei

**Key Thesis:** FMSH is a diversified Chinese IC design house where RFID/NFC is ONE product category within the broader "Security & Identification" segment (~22% of revenue, declining). FMSH's strategic capital and R&D are being directed toward FPGA (domestic substitution play), automotive MCU, and high-end security controllers --- NOT toward RFID/NFC growth. However, FMSH's HF RFID and NFC tag chips (FM11 series) are direct competitors to SIC in the China/ASEAN price-sensitive market. Critically, FMSH has NO LF animal identification products and NO NFC sensor interface products --- SIC's two strongest competitive moats remain unthreatened. The September 2025 US Entity List (FN4) designation creates both a constraint on FMSH's technology access and a reputational risk for SIC customers who source FMSH chips for export markets.

### 1.1 Six Critical Findings

1. **RFID/NFC is NOT FMSH's Growth Priority.** Security & Identification revenue declined 8.3% YoY to RMB 791M. FMSH's R&D capital (RMB 2B convertible bond) is earmarked for FPGA, SoC, NVM, Security Controller, and Passive IoT --- with no dedicated RFID/NFC investment line. RFID/NFC chips are a mature, price-competitive cash cow being maintained, not grown.

2. **Direct NFC Competition Exists via FM11 Series.** FMSH's FM11NT021 (NTAG213-compatible, NFC Type 2) and FM11NC08 (NFC channel chip, ISO 14443-A) compete directly with SIC's NFC tag and channel products. FM11RF08/32 (Mifare Classic-compatible) are among the most widely deployed HF RFID chips globally --- primarily in China's transit, campus card, and access control markets. These products compete on price, not features.

3. **No LF Animal ID Products --- SIC Moat Intact.** FMSH's product catalog contains ZERO low-frequency (125--134 kHz) animal identification ICs. No EM4205/4305 equivalent. No ISO 11784/11785 FDX-B products. SIC's entire LF animal ID portfolio (SIC278/279, SIC7150/51/52) faces no competition from FMSH.

4. **No NFC Sensor Interface --- SIC Moat Intact.** FMSH has no potentiostat, galvanostat, or NFC sensor interface ICs. No equivalent to SIC4340/41/43. FMSH's "sensor chips" reference is limited to RFID-integrated sensing (similar to EM's em|aura-sense), not dedicated electrochemical sensor interfaces.

5. **US Entity List (FN4) Creates Export Market Vulnerability.** FMSH was added to the US Entity List with FN4 (strictest designation) in September 2025. This blocks access to US EDA tools, fab equipment, and IP. For SIC, this creates an opportunity: customers exporting products containing FMSH chips to markets with US technology compliance requirements may need to de-risk their supply chain. SIC can position as a sanctions-clean alternative for NFC/RFID applications.

6. **"Passive IoT Basic Chip" R&D Project is the One to Watch.** Among FMSH's 5 funded R&D projects (via RMB 2B convertible bond), Project #5 --- "Passive IoT Basic Chip Development and Industrialization" --- is the most SIC-relevant. This likely targets battery-free IoT tags combining RFID/NFC with sensing capabilities. If commercialized, this could eventually compete with SIC's NFC energy harvesting products (SIC4310) and sensor interfaces. Timeline: 2--3 years out.

## 6. SIC Opportunity Assessment

### 6.1 Opportunity #1: Sanctions-Clean Alternative Positioning

**FMSH Position:** Added to US Entity List with FN4 (September 2025). This means:
- All US-origin technology exports to FMSH require licenses (presumption of denial)
- Customers embedding FMSH chips in products exported to US-aligned markets face compliance risk
- Some multinational OEMs may proactively de-risk supply chains by diversifying away from FMSH

**SIC Products:** All SIC products are sanctions-clean. SIC is Thailand-headquartered with BOI A1 promoted status. No US/EU/Japan sanctions exposure.

**Addressable Market:**

| Segment | Est. Affected TAM | SIC Target SAM | Assumptions |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|-------------|
| NFC tags for export products | $200M--$400M | $10--20M | Multinational OEMs sourcing from China for global distribution |
| HF RFID for international logistics | $300M--$500M | $5--15M | Supply chain traceability in US/EU-bound goods |
| Smart card chips for banking (export) | $500M--$800M | $0 | SIC does not compete in smart cards |
| Total (SIC-addressable) | $500M--$900M | $15--35M | Conservative; excludes China-domestic-only use |

**Recommended Actions:**
1. Develop a "sanctions-clean supply chain" message for customers currently using FMSH NFC/RFID chips in export-bound products
2. Target multinational OEMs with China manufacturing who need to certify their component supply chain for US/EU compliance
3. Engage Japanese and Korean electronics manufacturers who are actively de-risking from Chinese semiconductor suppliers
4. Position SIC's Thailand origin and BOI A1 status as a supply chain advantage

### 6.2 Opportunity #2: NFC Authentication (Technology Advantage)

**FMSH Position:** FM11NT021 is an NTAG213 clone with basic password protection. No dynamic URL generation confirmed, no HMAC or rolling code authentication. FMSH's NFC authentication approach relies on basic password and static UID --- adequate for China domestic anti-counterfeit but insufficient for high-security applications.

**SIC Position:** SIC43NT offers rolling code authentication with dynamic NDEF. SIC43S1 adds AES-128. Both provide meaningfully stronger security than FMSH's basic password approach.

**Head-to-Head:**

| Feature | FMSH FM11NT021 | SIC43NT | SIC43S1 | em|linq (EM4332) |
|---------|---------------|---------|---------|------------------|
| NFC Standard | Type 2 | Type 2/5 | Type 2/5 | Type 2 |
| Crypto | 32-bit password | Rolling code | AES-128 | HMAC-SHA1 (256-bit) |
| Dynamic URL | Not confirmed | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| App Required | — | No | No | No |
| Price Position | Lowest | Mid | Mid-high | Mid |
| Target Market | China domestic | Global | Global | Global (via Avery Dennison) |

**Assessment:** SIC43NT/S1 occupies a distinct position between FMSH's low-cost/low-security FM11NT021 and EM's mid-cost/standard-crypto em|linq. SIC's rolling code approach offers different security trade-offs than HMAC-SHA1, and SIC43S1's AES-128 matches or exceeds both competitors in cryptographic strength.

**Recommended Actions:**
1. Position SIC43NT/S1 as the "right-sized security" option --- stronger than FMSH's basic password, with different (potentially stronger) replay-attack resistance than EM's HMAC-SHA1
2. Target customers upgrading FROM FMSH FM11NT021 who need stronger authentication for export markets (luxury, wine/spirits, pharma)
3. Develop migration guides and pin-compatible reference designs where possible

### 6.3 Opportunity #3: NFC Sensor Interface (Unchallenged Moat)

**FMSH Position:** ZERO products in this category. No evidence of development in current R&D projects (Passive IoT project is 2--3 years away and may not include electrochemical sensing).

**SIC Products:** SIC4340 (galvanostat + NFC), SIC4341 (potentiostat + NFC), SIC4343 (voltage measurement + NFC). "First and only NFC + chemical sensor interface in market."

**Assessment:** This is SIC's strongest competitive moat against ALL competitors (FMSH, EM, NXP). Neither FMSH nor EM has an equivalent product. SIC should accelerate go-to-market:

| Segment | Est. Global TAM | SIC Target SAM | Notes |

| Industrial Sensing | $400M--$600M | $10--15M | Gas detection, water treatment |
| Total | $1.2B--$1.9B | $35--60M | No FMSH or EM competition |

### 6.4 Opportunity #4: LF Animal Identification (No FMSH Competition)

**FMSH Position:** ZERO LF animal ID products. FMSH's product catalog is entirely HF (13.56 MHz) and UHF (840--960 MHz). No ISO 11784/11785 compliance. No 125--134 kHz products of any kind.

**SIC Products:** SIC278/279 (world-class read range), SIC7150/51/52 (FDX-B), SIC824B (BLE 5.2 module for animal tracking).

## 8. Entity List / Sanctions Impact

### 8.1 FN4 Designation (September 2025)

FMSH was added to the US Department of Commerce Entity List with the strictest Footnote 4 (FN4) designation. Key implications:

| Impact Area | Detail | Confidence |
|------------|--------|------------|
| US EDA tools (Cadence, Synopsys, Mentor) | Licenses required; presumption of denial | ✅ Confirmed |
| US-origin fab equipment (Applied Materials, Lam, KLA) | Blocked for FMSH's foundry partners if dedicated to FMSH | ✅ Confirmed |
| ARM IP licensing | At risk; ARM is UK-based but uses US technology | ⚠️ Under review |
| Reason cited | Military modernization support; supplying Russian military end users | ✅ Per Commerce Dept. |
| FMSH Response | Claims "lawful and compliant" operations; pivoting to domestic EDA tools | ⚠️ Company statements |

### 8.2 Impact on FMSH's RFID/NFC Business

**Short-term (0--12 months):** Limited impact. FMSH's RFID/NFC products use mature process nodes (≥65nm) that are less affected by US equipment restrictions. Existing designs can continue production with current foundry partners. The annual report does NOT mention Entity List as a named risk (likely due to publication timing and legal sensitivity), but references "long-arm jurisdictional measures" in the macro-environmental risks section.

**Medium-term (12--36 months):** Moderate impact. If FMSH cannot access updated EDA tools for new NFC chip designs (including the Passive IoT project), product iteration speed will slow. Domestic EDA alternatives (Empyrean, X-EPIC) are improving but not yet at parity for complex mixed-signal designs.

**Long-term (3+ years):** Potentially significant. If restrictions tighten further, FMSH's ability to develop new RFID/NFC products with advanced security features or integrated sensing may be constrained. This widens SIC's technology gap in authentication and sensor interface.

### 8.3 SIC Implications

1. **Compliance messaging:** SIC can position as a sanctions-clean alternative for customers who need to certify their component supply chain for US/EU export compliance
2. **Customer de-risking:** Multinational OEMs with China manufacturing operations may proactively second-source away from FMSH for RFID/NFC components
3. **No direct benefit in China domestic:** Chinese customers serving the China-only market are unlikely to switch away from FMSH due to Entity List concerns
4. **Technology gap widening:** If FMSH's access to advanced design tools deteriorates, SIC's product development advantage in NFC authentication and sensor interface will increase over time

## 9. Predicted Strategic Moves (6--12 Months)

### Prediction 1: Continued RFID/NFC Price Aggression in China Domestic Market

**Confidence:** VERY HIGH (95%)

**Evidence:** Gross margin compression (61% → 56%), annual report explicitly cites "downward price adjustment" across product lines, Security & ID segment declining despite stable unit volumes. FMSH is trading margin for market share in mature RFID/NFC products.

**SIC Impact:** LOW for SIC directly (SIC's primary markets are outside China), but MEDIUM for ASEAN customers who also receive FMSH pricing pressure. SIC should NOT attempt to match FMSH on price for basic NFC tag ICs in China. Instead, compete on features (authentication, sensor interface) and on supply chain compliance.

### Prediction 2: Passive IoT Chip Development Announcement (H2 2026--2027)

**Confidence:** HIGH

**Evidence:** Named and funded R&D project (#5 in convertible bond allocation). FMSH has the RF and NVM technology base to build battery-free IoT tags. The China domestic market for electronic shelf labels and smart packaging is growing rapidly.

**Predicted Move:** FMSH will announce a Passive IoT chip combining NFC (likely ISO 14443-A or 15693) with basic sensing (temperature at minimum) and energy harvesting. Target markets: electronic shelf labels, cold chain monitoring, smart packaging.

**SIC Impact:** MEDIUM. If limited to temperature sensing and basic energy harvesting, this competes with EM's em|aura-sense and EM4325 more than with SIC's electrochemical sensor interface. If FMSH integrates potentiostat capability (unlikely in first generation), the threat escalates to HIGH.

### Prediction 3: Domestic EDA Tool Transition Accelerates

**Confidence:** HIGH

**Evidence:** FN4 designation forces FMSH to accelerate adoption of domestic EDA tools (Empyrean, X-EPIC, etc.). China's National Integrated Circuit Industry Fund has been investing in EDA development. FMSH's 2025 outlook explicitly references "paying active attention to overseas advanced technologies" and supply chain security.

**SIC Impact:** LOW. This is an internal FMSH challenge that does not directly affect SIC. However, if the transition slows FMSH's product development cycle, it indirectly benefits SIC by widening the technology gap in advanced NFC applications.

### Prediction 4: Automotive NFC/SE Push via Fuwei Xunjie

**Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH

**Evidence:** Annual report highlights: "vehicle on-board SE has made its way into Tier 1 of the automotive industry and is expected to be in mass production"; "digital key car regulation project has already been implemented"; FM17661A NFC Reader/Writer Chip won 2024 ICCE award; automotive MCU shipments exceeded 10M units.

**Predicted Move:** FMSH will aggressively push NFC-based digital car key and vehicle SE solutions in the China automotive market, leveraging existing Tier 1 relationships and the Fuwei Xunjie subsidiary's automotive test lab.

**SIC Impact:** LOW-MEDIUM. SIC's SIC61xx immobilizer family targets aftermarket; FMSH is targeting OEM digital keys. Different market segments, but FMSH's automotive SE could eventually compete if SIC expands into NFC-based vehicle access.

### Prediction 5: Security & ID Segment Revenue Continues Declining

**Confidence:** HIGH

**Evidence:** Bank card market saturation, social security card issuance cycle ending, price competition intensifying. No new RFID/NFC growth initiatives funded. All growth investment directed to FPGA, NVM, and MCU.

**SIC Impact:** POSITIVE for SIC. As FMSH deprioritizes Security & ID, technical support responsiveness and product innovation in RFID/NFC will slow --- creating openings for SIC to capture customers who need more responsive supplier engagement.
