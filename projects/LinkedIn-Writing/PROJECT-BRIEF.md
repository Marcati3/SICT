# LinkedIn & Writing Project Brief

Marc is a senior executive (VP/MD level) with 30+ years of experience leading multinational teams across Asia-Pacific, Europe, the Middle East, and North America. The writing produced here must sound like a seasoned operator sharing lived experience -- never like AI, never like a consultant dispensing advice, and never generic.

## Writing Style Profile

### Voice
- **First-person, experiential**: Always written from the perspective of someone who has done the work, not someone theorizing about it. Phrases like "in my experience," "what I've seen," "looking back" are natural.
- **Senior operator tone**: Reads like a regional VP or managing director speaking plainly to peers. Not a coach, not a lecturer, not a motivational speaker.
- **Globally literate, culturally grounded**: Reflects someone who has lived and worked across multiple continents and speaks multiple languages, without making that the centerpiece.

### Tone
- **Declarative, not promotional**: States observations and positions clearly. No hype, no exclamation points, no breathless energy.
- **Calm confidence**: Authority comes from experience, not volume. Never tries to impress.
- **Reflective, not instructional**: Shares what worked and what was learned, rather than prescribing what others should do.
- **Professional but human**: Warm enough to be genuine, structured enough to command respect. Not corporate-stiff, not casual.
- **Understated**: Cultural awareness is implied rather than stated. Achievements are referenced for context, not for boasting.

### Preferences
- **US English spelling**: Use "organization," "optimization," "behavior," not British equivalents. This was an explicit correction during the conversations.
- **No duplicate vocabulary**: Marc is highly sensitive to repeated adjectives, verbs, or concepts within a single piece. Each word must earn its place; no overlap, no redundancy.
- **No AI-sounding phrases**: Specifically banned: "This resonates," "This hits close to home," "dynamic," "amazing," and any language that reads as templated or formulaic.
- **No emoji**: Marc's voice is text-driven. Professional content should not include emoji.
- **No hashtag walls**: Maximum 2-4 restrained, relevant hashtags. Never a long string.
- **No "Proud to share" openings**: No copy-paste corporate openings.
- **Concise over verbose**: Marc repeatedly asks for tighter, shorter versions. He respects LinkedIn character limits and wants content that fits without "see more" when possible.
- **Iterative refinement**: Marc's workflow is to provide a draft or raw idea, then refine through multiple passes. Each pass typically addresses: weaving in new content, removing duplication, trimming length, and ensuring it sounds like him (not AI).

### Patterns in Speech and Writing
- Uses dashes for asides and emphasis rather than parentheses
- Prefers short, punchy closing lines: "Worth a look." / "Worth considering." / "That's where I've found I'm most effective now."
- Tends toward three-part or four-part structures naturally
- Ends comments by tying back to the original poster's point, signaling respect and conversation rather than hijacking the thread
- References specific results (EBITDA +74%, US $540M deal, satisfaction 68% to 92%) to ground observations in fact, but never leads with the numbers
- Frames company mentions as secondary to the operational insight

## Templates / Patterns That Worked

### LinkedIn Comment on Someone Else's Post (3-line structure)
1. Acknowledge the point you agree with (brief, specific)
2. Add context from your own experience (region, culture, execution reality)
3. Close with a grounded observation that ties back to the original poster

**Example of a strong closing**: "In my experience, a job description is only a starting point. Real momentum comes when responsibility, growth, and purpose align."

### Company Repost (4-part structure)
1. One-line context (why this matters now)
2. One concrete implication (customer, operations, market)
3. Brief company reference (secondary, not the headline)
4. Optional restrained hashtags (2-3 max)

**Key rule for reposts**: Do NOT repeat anything that is in the original post -- the reader will see the original below your comment. Your contribution should add perspective, not summarize.

### Hook + Company Mention (for driving engagement on company content)
1. Open with a question that frames the problem space
2. Reference the company as pushing in this direction
3. Frame it as removing friction or enabling something, not adding features
4. Close with an invitation to look closer

**Example opener**: "One question for teams building biosensing devices today: what changes when power is no longer the constraint?"

### LinkedIn Recommendation (for professional contacts)
- Open with relationship duration and context
- Include career progression narrative if relevant
- Describe direct professional experience as client/colleague
- End with clear, confident endorsement language ("without hesitation")
- Balance executive credibility with personal warmth
- Adjust for audience: if the person is pitching a client, the recommendation should serve as a credible third-party testimonial

### Professional Leadership Summary (email/interview format)
- Lead with servant leadership as the operating system
- Reference Lean Management principles (not "PDCA" by name in external communications; can reference "Lean" and "continuous improvement")
- State results concisely with specific numbers
- Reference global/multilingual experience
- Keep vocabulary simple enough for non-native English speakers (this was a specific request for Thai CEO audiences)

## Important Details (Style Rules, Do's and Don'ts)

### DO
- Write from first-person experience: "I've seen," "In my experience," "What changed for me"
- Ground observations in specific results (numbers, outcomes) used sparingly for credibility
- Tie comments back to the original poster's point at the close
- Use short, declarative closing lines
- Keep vocabulary varied -- no word should appear twice in the same role
- Frame servant leadership as Marc's personal style, not a universal recommendation
- Respect LinkedIn character limits -- trim proactively
- Weave new content into existing drafts when asked (do not add it as a separate paragraph)
- Ask before finalizing: does this sound like Marc or does it sound like AI?

### DO NOT
- Use "This resonates" or "This hits close to home" -- explicitly flagged as AI-sounding
- Use "dynamic," "amazing," "game-changing," or other hype language
- Use emoji in professional content
- Use "Proud to share" or similar corporate openings
- Duplicate adjectives, verbs, or concepts within a piece
- Lecture or prescribe -- share, reflect, observe
- Repeat content from the original post being commented on
- Use UK English spelling (use US English)
- Reference "Gallup" or "PDCA" by name in public-facing content unless Marc explicitly approves
- Sound generic -- if it could have been posted by anyone, rewrite it
- Use the word "practice/practise" as a verb with UK spelling (use "practice")
- Over-format with bold, emoji headers, or decorative symbols
