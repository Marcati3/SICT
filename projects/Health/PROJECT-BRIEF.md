# Health Project Brief

## Project Overview

This project tracks the health management journey of a 58-year-old male (177 cm, 74 kg, BMI ~23.6) focused on two primary goals: **weight loss / body fat optimization** and **slowing the aging process**. The individual is active (plays ice hockey 1-2 times per week for 90 minutes), practices intermittent fasting (eating lunch and dinner only, 16:8 pattern), and is managing multiple medical interventions simultaneously.

The conversation spans from July 2025 through February 2026 and covers pharmaceutical weight loss therapy, HGH optimization, sleep apnea management with CPAP/APAP, blood work monitoring, and side effect management.

## Key Decisions Made

### Medications

1. **Started Ozempic (semaglutide)** off-label for weight loss (non-diabetic use), beginning around August 2025.
   - Titration: 0.25 mg weekly for the first month, with plan to escalate to 0.5 mg, then 1.0 mg.
   - Stayed at 0.25 mg for 2 months, extended due to a 2-week travel break.
   - Decided against jumping to 0.5 mg after the break; chose to repeat 0.25 mg for 2-4 more weeks first.

2. **Switched from Ozempic to Mounjaro (tirzepatide)** around November 2025.
   - Started at 2.5 mg, then escalated to 5 mg.
   - Experienced severe GI side effects at 5 mg (7 days of diarrhea, sulfur burps, dehydration).
   - Decision: **dropped back to 2.5 mg** for at least 4 weeks before reattempting 5 mg.
   - Last 5 mg injection was November 23, 2025. Restarted at 2.5 mg around December 5, 2025.
   - As of February 2026, still on Mounjaro 2.5 mg weekly.

3. **HGH (Human Growth Hormone)** - pre-existing therapy, adjusted during this period.
   - Was on 2.31 IU daily, 7 days per week.
   - Baseline IGF-1: 191 ng/mL.
   - After labs showed IGF-1 elevated to 328 ng/mL (supra-physiologic), decided to reduce.
   - Decision: reduce to **2.0 IU daily** (targeting IGF-1 of 250-300 ng/mL for longevity).
   - Also trialed 6 days/week dosing as a gentler step-down.
   - Dosing time: morning, on empty stomach (fasted due to IF).

### Sleep Apnea / CPAP

4. **APAP settings adjusted** from 5-15 cmH2O to **6-15 cmH2O** (raised minimum pressure).
   - EPR: 3 (kept at 3; considered reducing to 2 if SpO2 did not improve).
   - Humidity: reduced from 5 to **3** (reduced drooling and mouth leaks), with some toggling to 4.
   - Start pressure raised from 4 to 6 cmH2O.
   - Ramp time: 5 minutes.

5. **Mask**: ResMed AirFit N30i nasal cradle mask.
   - Persistent leak issues throughout the period.
   - Ordered a new nasal pillow cushion in December 2025 to troubleshoot.
   - Decision: keep current mask type, focus on fit optimization rather than switching.

### Lifestyle

6. **Diet**: intermittent fasting (lunch and dinner only), high protein target of ~130-135 g/day (1.8 g/kg).
7. **Exercise**: ice hockey 1-2x/week, recommendation to add 2x/week resistance training and 1x/week Zone 2 cardio.
8. **Rejected lymphatic drainage supplements** as ineffective and potentially harmful given dehydration risk.

## Medication Interaction Notes

- **Mounjaro + HGH**: No direct drug interaction. Opposing metabolic effects: Mounjaro lowers blood glucose while HGH raises it. Mounjaro suppresses appetite while HGH may increase it. The combination can work well for fat loss + lean mass preservation if protein intake is maintained at ~130-135 g/day.
- **Mounjaro + Alcohol**: Avoid alcohol 48-72 hours after injection (worsens GI side effects and OSA).
- **HGH + OSA**: HGH can worsen sleep apnea by thickening upper-airway soft tissue and causing fluid shifts. Must monitor CPAP AHI and SpO2 when adjusting HGH dose.
- **Mounjaro + Kidney function**: Dehydration from GI side effects can cause acute kidney stress. Hydration of 2-2.5 L/day is critical.

## Key Protocols Established

### Hydration Protocol
- 2-2.5 L water per day (minimum)
- Electrolytes 2-3x per week
- Avoid alcohol for 48-72 hours after Mounjaro injection
- Limit coffee to 1-2 cups, avoid on injection day if possible

### Nutrition Protocol
- Protein: ~130-135 g/day (1.8 g/kg), split between lunch and dinner
- Sources: chicken breast, salmon, eggs, Greek yogurt, whey protein isolate
- Carbs: moderate, timed around hockey/training days
- Fats: olive oil, avocado, fatty fish, nuts
- Soluble fiber: psyllium husk, chia seeds (for bowel regularity and cholesterol)

### CPAP Monitoring Protocol (Weekly)
- 7-day AHI (target < 2)
- SpO2 average (target >= 94%) and minutes < 90%
- 95th percentile pressure (flag if >= 14.5 cmH2O)
- 95th percentile leak rate (target < 24 L/min)
- Leak graph timing (correlate events with leaks)

### Lab Monitoring Schedule
- **Every 3-6 months**: IGF-1, fasting glucose/insulin, HbA1c, CMP (creatinine, eGFR, liver enzymes), lipid panel, urinalysis, electrolytes
- **Next labs should include**: ApoB and hs-CRP (not yet tested)
- **Thyroid panel**: annually (TSH, Free T4, Free T3)

### CPAP Adjustment Decision Rules
- If AHI > 5 or rises >= 3 above treated baseline for > 1 week: investigate
- If 95th percentile pressure >= 14.5: discuss raising max pressure with sleep physician
- If SpO2 average drops below 92% or time < 90% > 15 min/night: trial EPR 2, consider higher minimum pressure
- If leak > 24 L/min at 95th percentile consistently: troubleshoot mask fit before adjusting any other settings
