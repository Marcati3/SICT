# Personal Finance Project Brief

## Project Overview

This project tracks an ongoing personal financial strategy for a Thai citizen managing a globally diversified investment portfolio through Interactive Brokers (IBKR). The investor holds assets denominated in USD and HKD, with banking relationships in Thailand (KBank, UOB Zenith credit card) and an overseas brokerage. The overarching goal is to grow the portfolio from its current level to **$2,000,000 USD within 5 years** (target date approximately 2030).

**Investor Profile:**
- Thai citizen, resident in Thailand
- Uses IBKR as primary brokerage (self-directed, loads orders manually)
- Banking: Kasikorn Bank (KBank) for THB and USD; UOB Zenith credit card for daily purchases
- Currencies held: USD and HKD only (all positions denominated in one of these two)
- Risk tolerance: Growth-seeking, high-return focused, but disciplined (no leverage, staged entries)
- Investment style: Buy-and-hold compounder with tactical limit orders; 70/30 scalable-vs-premium framework

## Key Decisions Made

### Core Investment Strategy: 70/30 Scalable vs Premium

The investor adopted a **70% scalable growth / 30% premium monopoly leaders** allocation framework:

- **Scalable names (70%):** Lower-priced, easier to build positions, dividend-generating. Includes Samsung (SMSD), Rio Tinto (RIO), Sea Ltd (SE), AMD, TSM.
- **Premium leaders (30%):** Category-defining monopolies with structural moats. Includes ASML, Novo Nordisk (NVO), MercadoLibre (MELI).

### Order Execution Discipline

- **Staged entries:** Deploy ~50% at market, ~50% via GTC limit orders at 5-15% below current prices.
- **Order types:** Use **midprice orders** for expensive/illiquid stocks (MELI, ASML, Samsung GDR, NVO); **market orders** for highly liquid names (AMD); **GTC limit orders** for all staged dip buys.
- **Dividend reinvestment:** All dividends reinvested into whichever premium names are closest to support levels.

### Position-Specific Decisions

1. **NVDA (Nvidia):** Hold existing position (~200 shares). Decided NOT to add more at current all-time highs. Revisit only on 10-15% dip or when future cash arrives.
2. **TSM (TSMC):** Added as core semiconductor foundry exposure -- fills the "missing middle" between Nvidia (design) and ASML (tooling). Initial buy ~22 shares at ~$270.
3. **SE (Sea Ltd):** Built position across three lots to 100 shares total. Average cost basis: ~$168.55/share (Lot 1: 25 shares @ $186.13; Lot 2: 25 shares @ $175.00; Lot 3: 50 shares @ $156.54).
4. **SMSD (Samsung GDR Preferred):** Bought 12 shares total (6 initial + 6 more), larger than originally planned. Listed on LSE as SMSD.L.
5. **CVX (Chevron):** Decided to HOLD despite flat price performance. Role is cash-flow anchor, volatility dampener, and psychological ballast -- not a growth driver. ~4% dividend yield. Will become a rotation/funding source only when portfolio exceeds $1.1-1.3M.
6. **Boeing (BA):** Hold as cyclical play. Not a growth engine. Consider trimming later if capital needed elsewhere.
7. **Limit orders reduced over time:** SMSD limit reduced from 6 to 3 shares; TSM limit reduced from 20 to 10 shares -- both to manage liquidity given low cash reserves.

### Banking and Money Movement Decisions

- **Offshore banking:** Evaluated Singapore, Hong Kong, Channel Islands (HSBC Expat), and other jurisdictions. Singapore ranked #1 for benefit/ease trade-off for a Thai citizen. No final action taken yet.
- **Crypto as remittance rail:** Investigated using Paxos/Zero Hash through IBKR for money transfers. Concluded: IBKR allows crypto withdrawal to external wallet (via Paxos/Zero Hash), but does NOT accept crypto deposits. Funding must be fiat-only. For actual crypto remittance, need separate exchange (Binance, Bitkub, etc.).
- **IBKR funding path:** THB -> KBank FX wallet -> USD -> wire to IBKR (direct fiat path). Estimated cost ~$90-135 per $5K transfer.
