Here’s a clear breakdown — **“market” vs “midprice” orders** sound similar but behave *very* differently inside IBKR. This is worth understanding before placing trades:  

---

## 🟢 Market Order — Fast Execution, Less Price Control

**What it is:**  
A market order tells IBKR: *“Buy (or sell) immediately at the best available price.”*  

- ✅ **Speed:** Almost always executes instantly during market hours.  
- ✅ **Certainty:** You *will* get filled.  
- ⚠️ **Downside:** You might pay more (for a buy) or receive less (for a sell) if the bid-ask spread is wide or liquidity is thin.  
- 📉 **Example:**  
  - Best ask: \$302.40  
  - You place a market buy → you get filled instantly at \$302.40 (or slightly higher if the next shares are higher).  

**Best for:**  
- Highly liquid, large-cap stocks (e.g., TSM, AMD).  
- When you *must* enter immediately (e.g., earnings event, breakout).  

---

## ⚖️ Midprice Order — Price Efficiency, Less Execution Certainty

**What it is:**  
A midprice order tells IBKR: *“Try to fill me at the midpoint between the current bid and ask.”*  

- ✅ **Better pricing:** Usually fills at a slightly better price than a market order.  
- ✅ **Spread advantage:** Especially useful for wide-spread stocks like ASML or MELI.  
- ⚠️ **Downside:** May *not fill immediately* or at all if the market moves away from your mid.  
- ⚠️ **Execution speed:** Can be slower — especially in fast-moving markets.  
- 📉 **Example:**  
  - Bid: \$302.00 / Ask: \$302.40  
  - Midpoint: \$302.20  
  - You place a midprice buy → you *might* get filled around \$302.20 if sellers meet you there.  

**Best for:**  
- Less-liquid or wide-spread stocks (ASML, MELI, Samsung GDR).  
- When you’re *not in a rush* and want slightly better entry pricing.  
- Scaling into large positions over time.

---

### 📊 Side-by-Side Comparison

| Feature | **Market Order** | **Midprice Order** |
|--------|------------------|--------------------|
| Execution Speed | 🔥 Fastest (instant) | ⚖️ Slower / not guaranteed |
| Fill Certainty | ✅ Always fills | ⚠️ May not fill |
| Price Control | ❌ Worst (take what market gives) | ✅ Better (often midpoint) |
| Best Use | Must-enter trades, very liquid stocks | Tight execution, thin or wide-spread stocks |
| Risk | Slippage (bad fill) | No fill (missed trade) |

---

✅ **Pro Tip for Your Strategy:**  
- Use **market orders** for high-liquidity positions where timing matters (e.g., TSM, AMD, NVO).  
- Use **midprice orders** for expensive or thinly traded names (ASML, MELI, Samsung GDR) to save on entry cost and reduce slippage — even 0.5% better fill on a \$2,400 MELI share is meaningful.
